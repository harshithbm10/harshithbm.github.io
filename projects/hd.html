Digital Documentation of heritage site in Kolar

Our project titled " In the shadows of the rocks" Shortlisted for Heritage Documentation Awards 2018 by CoA India (Top 5 entries Nationwide).

Objective

  1. To identify and document historically significant structures that are unrecognized by Archaeological Survey of India (ASI).
  2. Develop a digitally accurate drawings and details to be able to carry renovation or re-construction activities if required.
  3. Accurate detailing of measurements and representation of features to be able to easily interpreted.

Methodology

  1. Acquire data and link with respective measurements.
  2. Preprocess the image by orthorectification, feature identification.
  3. Generate and tag control points as measured.
  4. Stitch neighborhood images together and generate a overall feature map.
  5. Generate feature map or detail map within the bounded control points.
  6. Process the drawings into a AutoCad document.
  7. Random checking to ensure measurements are within tolerance.

Concept of Photogramettry borrowed from Satellite imaging.

  • We satarted with traditional techniques used in heritage documentation of measuring (in the absence of lidar application) of measuring sketching and drafting features in relavant softwares.
  • This process involved a lot of dependencies and had the first hand experience of the axiom of measurement uncertainty.
  • which states : "no measurement can be absolutely accurate and with every measurement there is some finite uncertainty about measured value or measured attribute."
  • An incident that underscored this inherent uncertainty occurred when a friend fell while measuring details over 12 feet above the ground. This unfortunate event prompted a search for the nearest hospital using Google Maps, sparking curiosity about the precision with which satellite images are captured.
  • This was our light-bulb moment with team-mates falling sick as days went and now with a team member being injured we realised the only way forward is to research the techniques in satellite imaging and replicate in our process.
  • After relevant research we implemented photogrammetry techniques for acquisition, orthorectification, segregation, data interpretation, and analysis, used in the domain of satellite imaging resulting in accurate heritage documentation.
